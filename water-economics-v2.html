Observer's conclusion

The framing of water as an environmental problem has allowed the world to treat it as someone else's concern — a charity issue, a climate statistic, a UN goal perpetually off track. The data does not support that framing. When the Bank for International Settlements models water scarcity alongside inflation risk, when hedge funds call it the biggest emerging market on earth, when the IMF projects 8% GDP losses by 2050 — this has crossed from environmental to macroeconomic. The institutions that manage global economic stability are now pricing water risk. The question is whether the rest of the world catches up before the supply-demand gap does.
